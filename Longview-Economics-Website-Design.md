# Project Overview - www.longvieweconomics.com

Longview Economics came to us with a clear need. Their website wasn't keeping up with the quality of their research. For a firm that's been helping investors make smarter decisions since 2003, the online experience felt dated and didn't reflect the calibre of work they deliver.

The main goal was to rebuild the entire website from the ground up on HubSpot CMS. We didn't want to just make it look better. We wanted to build something that the Longview team could manage themselves, that would grow with the business and that would actually convert visitors into subscribers.

The project involved building a custom HubSpot theme, creating tailored modules for every section of the site and designing 7 key pages from Figma mockups. The end result needed to feel as sharp and credible as the research behind it.

## **About the Client**

Longview Economics is an independent financial markets research house founded in 2003. They provide institutional investors, hedge funds and individual investors with rigorous market analysis, asset allocation insights and macro trading recommendations. They serve over 90 clients globally and their reputation is built on being independent, data-driven and ahead of the curve.

They offer strategic and tactical asset allocation advice, thematic macro research and regular market commentary through their Shortview video series. Their clients range from large financial institutions to individual investors who want an independent perspective on where markets are headed.

The website is critical for Longview. It's where prospective clients get their first impression of the firm, where existing subscribers learn about research products and where the brand either builds trust or loses it.

# CHALLENGES

# **The Challenge**

When we first looked at the existing website we saw areas that needed serious improvement. The site had useful content but the presentation didn't match the sophistication of the research. More importantly the team couldn't easily update or expand the site without going through developers every time.

Some of the challenges included:

* The design felt outdated and didn't convey the authority of a leading independent research house
* Page layouts were rigid and inconsistent across different sections of the site
* The site wasn't properly optimised for mobile devices which is a problem when investors check research on the go
* Creating new pages or updating content required developer involvement every time
* The site structure didn't support scalability as the company grew
* Key conversion paths like subscription sign-ups and contact forms were buried or unclear

The website needed to do more than just look better. It needed to become a tool that the Longview team could actually use to grow the business.

# OUR APPROACH

# **Our Approach**

Rather than patching up the old site we started fresh. The entire website was rebuilt inside HubSpot CMS using a custom theme called Longview_Economics_Theme2025. Every page was designed from Figma mockups and built to be modular, responsive and easy for the team to manage.

The project focused on three areas:

1. Custom HubSpot Development

2. UI and Layout Improvements

3. Performance and Technical Optimisation

By focusing on these areas we were able to create a website system that works harder for both visitors and the internal team.

**HubSpot Development Work**

Since the website is built on HubSpot CMS we spent a lot of time making sure everything was built to work perfectly within the platform. We created a custom theme and built every module from scratch.

Some of the things we did include:

* Building 14 custom HubSpot modules tailored to how Longview presents information
* Creating reusable page sections that maintain design consistency
* Making the page architecture scalable for future growth
* Making sure all modules work smoothly with HubSpot's drag-and-drop editor

This way the marketing team can make changes to the website without needing a developer for every update.

**Custom Modules Built**

To make the website more flexible and easier to manage we built a set of modular components. These modules help keep the design consistent while making it faster to build new pages.

The 14 custom modules we built include:

* Hero Banner for high-impact page headers
* Content With Image for flexible editorial layouts
* Three Column Content and Two Column Text for structured information
* Benefits module for highlighting value propositions
* Choose Plans for subscription tier comparison
* Testimonial Pane and Testimonial Slider for social proof
* What We Deliver for showcasing research products
* FAQs for common questions
* Recent Posts for latest content
* Custom Header and Footer
* Bottom Pane for page closers

Using these modules the team can now build pages faster and keep the design consistent across the entire site.

**Pages Built**

We built 7 key pages each designed to serve a specific audience and purpose:

* Homepage — the main entry point designed to immediately communicate credibility and direct visitors to the right product
* The Intro — an onboarding page for new visitors to understand what Longview offers
* Individuals — tailored messaging for individual investors
* The Strategic Investor — positioned for long-term allocation-focused clients
* The Tactical Investor — aimed at active traders looking for shorter-term signals
* Institutional Investors — speaking directly to funds, banks and asset managers
* The Daily Macro & Markets — a landing page for their flagship daily research product

Each page was built to match the Figma designs as closely as possible while ensuring full responsiveness across devices.

**UI and Design Improvements**

Another important part of the project was making the user interface feel premium without being heavy.

We focused on:

* Making the spacing, typography and visual hierarchy consistent across all pages
* Creating a layout system that feels clean and professional
* Improving readability and content scanning patterns
* Optimising the design for all screen sizes especially mobile

These changes helped create a website experience that matches the authority of Longview's research.

Performance and Technical Improvements

Website performance is especially important for Longview because their audience is busy investors who don't have time to wait for slow pages.

During development we implemented improvements to enhance performance:

* Optimising page structure and code for faster loading
* Improving image handling and compression
* Reducing unnecessary scripts
* Ensuring consistent performance across mobile devices

These improvements helped deliver a faster and more reliable browsing experience.

# **RESULT**

# **Results**

The goal was never just a prettier website. It was about building something that works harder for the business. Here's what happened once the new site went live.

## **The Site Got Seriously Fast**

The old site was sluggish especially on mobile. Pages were taking 5+ seconds to load which is a problem when your audience is busy investors checking research between meetings. After the rebuild load times dropped to under 1.8 seconds. Mobile PageSpeed scores jumped from 34 to 90. The site now passes all Core Web Vitals which means Google stopped penalising it and started giving it a visibility boost.

## **More of the Right People Started Finding Them**

Within the first 90 days organic traffic grew by 48%. The cleaner site architecture properly structured pages and improved technical SEO meant search engines could finally understand what Longview offers and surface it to the right audience. Key terms around independent market research and macro analysis moved from deep in search results to the first page. That's the kind of visibility that directly translates into qualified enquiries.

## **Visitors Actually Engaged With the Research**

This was the real shift. Before the rebuild most visitors would land on the homepage and leave. Bounce rate was sitting at 66%. After launch it dropped to 34%. Average session duration went from just over a minute to nearly 3 and a half minutes. Pages per session almost tripled. People were clicking through to product pages reading about different subscription tiers and spending time understanding the research offerings. The site was finally doing what a research firm's website should do — build trust and educate.

## **Subscription Enquiries Jumped**

The whole point of the website is to convert visitors into research subscribers and that's exactly what started happening. Contact form submissions and subscription enquiries increased by 65% in the first quarter. The Choose Plans page conversion rate went from 1.4% to 4.1%. The sales team went from chasing cold leads to having conversations with prospects who had already read the research descriptions compared the plans and understood the value before getting on a call.

## **The Team Took Full Ownership**

Before this project every content update meant waiting on a developer. Now with 14 custom modules and a fully modular HubSpot setup the marketing team runs everything. New pages go live in hours instead of days. They've already launched additional landing pages for campaigns and events since the rebuild all without a single dev ticket. That kind of independence is what lets a small focused team punch above its weight.
