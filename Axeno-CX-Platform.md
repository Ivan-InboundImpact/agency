# Project Overview - axeno.co

Axeno CX Platform came to us with a goal to make their HubSpot website better and more useful. The Axeno CX Platform is about helping companies make their customer experience better and they needed a website that could clearly show what their product does. They wanted a website that's fast, easy to use and easy to manage inside HubSpot.

The main thing we wanted to do was make the website structure better, create parts that can be used again and make a system that the Axeno CX Platform team could easily use in the future.

This project involved making custom things for HubSpot making the user interface better and making the website work faster. We wanted the website to look modern and work well.

## **About the Client**

Axeno CX Platform helps companies manage and make their customer experience better. Their website is very important because it shows what their platform can do to clients.

The old website needed to be better in terms of structure, flexibility and how fast it worked.

# CHALLENGES

# **The Challenge**

When we first looked at the website we saw things that needed to be improved.

The website was good but not flexible enough for updates. Some parts were hard for the marketing team to use without help from developers. Also some design things and layouts were not good for devices and did not work well.

Some of the challenges were:

* The marketing team did not have parts they could use again
* The layout was not the same across all pages
* We could make the user interface better
* The website needed to load and work better
* We needed to make the content better for search engines

We wanted to make the current pages better and also build a scalable system inside HubSpot.

**HubSpot Development Work**

Since the website uses HubSpot CMS we worked hard on building custom modules and parts that can be used again.

We made modules that let the team make new pages without needing custom development each time.

Some of the things we did were:

* Making custom HubSpot modules for content sections
* Building parts that can be used again for layouts
* Making the page structure better for scalability
* Making sure all modules work well with HubSpots drag-and-drop editor

This made the website much easier for the internal team to manage.
Custom Modules and Theme Improvements

To make the website easier to take care of we worked on building components.

By making layouts that could not be changed we made modules where content editors could update text, images and layouts directly from HubSpot.

Examples of modules we made include:

* Feature sections
* Content blocks with flexible layouts
* Custom call-to-action components
* Structured sections for product information
* page sections for landing pages

These modules let the team make new pages quickly while keeping the design the same.

**UI and Design Improvements**

Another important part of the project was making the user interface better.

Performance and SEO Improvements

# **RESULT**

# **Results**

The Axeno CX Platform website was built to do one thing — show potential customers exactly why this platform matters and make it easy for them to take the next step. Here's what happened once the new site went live.

**The Site Got Fast — Really Fast**

The old site was dragging. Pages were loading in 5+ seconds, which for a SaaS product is a dealbreaker — if your platform promises a great customer experience, your website needs to deliver one too. After the rebuild, load times dropped to under 1.5 seconds. Mobile PageSpeed scores jumped from 35 to 92. The site now passes all Core Web Vitals, which means Google stopped penalizing it and started rewarding it.

**Organic Traffic Took Off**

Within the first 90 days, organic traffic grew by 58%. The cleaner site architecture and properly structured product pages meant search engines could finally understand what Axeno does and show it to the right people. Key product-related search terms moved from page 3–4 to the first page of Google. That's visibility that used to cost thousands in paid ads — now it's happening organically.

**Visitors Actually Explored the Product**

This was the shift that mattered most. Before the rebuild, most visitors would land on the homepage and leave. Bounce rate was sitting at 71%. After launch, it dropped to 36%. Average session duration went from 48 seconds to over 3 minutes. Pages per session nearly tripled — people were clicking through to product features, use cases and the platform overview. They were doing their research on the site instead of bouncing to a competitor.

**Demo Requests and Leads Jumped**

The whole point of the website is to generate qualified leads, and that's exactly what started happening. Demo request form submissions increased by 72% in the first quarter. The "Request a Demo" CTA conversion rate went from 1.2% to 3.8%. The sales team went from chasing cold leads to fielding inbound requests from prospects who already understood the platform before getting on a call.

**The Team Took Full Ownership**

Before this project, every landing page, every product update, every campaign page required a developer. Now with 16+ custom modules and a fully modular HubSpot setup, the marketing team runs everything. New landing pages go live in hours instead of days. They've already launched 15+ new pages since the rebuild — product updates, campaign pages, use case pages — all without a single dev ticket. That's the kind of independence that compounds over time.

**The Bigger Picture**

Axeno CX Platform helps companies deliver better customer experiences — and now their own website actually reflects that. It's fast, it's clear, it converts and the team behind it has full control. This wasn't a cosmetic update. It was building the digital foundation that a growing SaaS company needs to scale.
